Cast Iron Ribeye — Sear & Bake

Vegetable Side

  • Halved or quartered bok choy tucks in the skillet alongside the steak — rides through the sear and oven, comes out crispy on the cut sides.
  • Also good: broccolini, asparagus, halved baby peppers. Skip watery veg (zucchini, mushrooms) — they'll steam, not char.

Thawing (if frozen)

  • 1" steak: 12–18 hours in fridge.
  • 1.5" ribeye: 24 hours in fridge.
  • 2"+ steak: 36 hours in fridge.
  • Quick thaw: sealed bag in cold water on the counter, change water every 30 min, ~1 hour. Never thaw uncovered on counter.

Prep (30–45 min before)

  • Pull steak from fridge, pat very dry with paper towels.
  • Salt generously on both sides. Let sit at room temp 30–45 min.
  • Preheat oven to 400°F.

Sear

  • Heat cast iron over high heat 4–5 min, until just smoking. Crack a window / hood fan on.
  • Add a thin film of high–smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed).
  • Lay steak in away from you. Don't move it. Sear 2 min.
  • Flip, sear other side 2 min.
  • If thick: stand on fat cap edge with tongs ~30 sec.

Finish in Oven

  • Transfer the whole skillet to the 400°F oven.
  • Pull at internal temp (use a thermometer, not the clock):
120°FRare
125–130°FMedium-Rare
135°FMedium
  • Usually 3–6 min, depending on thickness.

Rest

  • Move steak to a plate or board. Tent loosely with foil.
  • Rest 8–10 min. Non-negotiable — juicy vs. puddle.
  • Pepper after resting (pepper burns in the sear).

Optional: Butter Baste

  • In the last minute of stovetop searing, add:
  • A knob of butter, a smashed garlic clove, a sprig of thyme or rosemary.
  • Tilt the pan, spoon foaming butter over the steak repeatedly.
Tip: rule of thumb — thicker steaks need more oven time, less stovetop. A 1.5"+ ribeye benefits from the full sear-then-bake. A 1" steak may need no oven at all.
