Fresh Sous Vide Ribeye + Cast Iron Sear

Vegetable Side

  • Skip the bath - too low and slow for veg. Sear bok choy (halved), broccolini, or asparagus in the cast iron during the steak's rest.
  • 2–3 min in the rendered fat = crispy, charred. Skip watery veg (zucchini, mushrooms).

Prep

  • Season steak with salt (and any aromatics - garlic, thyme, rosemary).
  • Vacuum seal, or use a Ziploc with the water-displacement method.
  • Set water bath to target temp (see below).

Bath Temperatures

125°FRare
129–131°FMedium-Rare
134°FMedium

Cook in Bath

  • Drop sealed bag in once bath hits target temp.
  • 1" steak: 1 hour minimum, up to 2 hours.
  • 1.5" ribeye: 1.5 hours minimum, up to 3 hours.
  • 2"+ steak: 2 hours minimum, up to 4 hours.
  • Going past max time = mushy texture. Don't.

Sear (the critical step)

  • Pull bag from bath. Remove steak.
  • Pat EXTREMELY dry with paper towels. This is the difference between a real crust and a steamed mess.
  • Heat cast iron over high heat 4–5 min, until smoking.
  • Thin film of high–smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed).
  • Sear 45–60 sec per side. That's it. Steak is already at temp.
  • Optional: stand on fat cap edge ~30 sec with tongs.

Optional: Butter Baste

  • In the last 30 sec of searing, add a knob of butter, smashed garlic, sprig of thyme/rosemary.
  • Tilt pan, spoon foaming butter over steak.

Rest

  • Short rest only - 2–3 min.
  • Steak is already at temp throughout. Long rest just makes it cool.
  • Pepper after resting.
Tip: sous vide is the most forgiving method - once steak is in the bath, it can wait. Use this when dinner timing is uncertain, or when cooking multiple steaks for a group.
