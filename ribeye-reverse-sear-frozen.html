Oven Reverse Sear Ribeye (Frozen)

Vegetable Side

  • Skip the low oven phase — too cool to char. Toss bok choy (halved), broccolini, or asparagus into the cast iron during the steak's rest after the hard sear.
  • 2–3 min in the rendered fat = crispy, charred edges. Skip watery veg (zucchini, mushrooms).
No thawing, no sous vide. Frozen steak straight into a low oven, hard sear at the end. The "I forgot to thaw and don't want to wait" method.

Prep

  • Unwrap frozen steak. No need to thaw.
  • Pat any surface frost off with paper towels.
  • Salt generously on both sides — salt sticks fine to frozen surface.
  • Preheat oven to 250°F.

Low and Slow in Oven

  • Place steak on wire rack over a sheet pan.
  • Insert thermometer probe (once steak softens enough — usually 15 min in).
  • Cook until internal temp is ~10–15°F below target.
105°FRare
110–115°FMedium-Rare
120°FMedium
  • 1" steak: ~45–60 min.
  • 1.5" ribeye: ~75–90 min.
  • 2"+ steak: ~100–120 min.
  • Use the thermometer, not the clock. Frozen times are roughly 1.5–2x fresh.

Rest Briefly

  • Pull from oven. Let steak rest 5–10 min while you heat the skillet.
  • Pat the surface extremely dry — frozen-cooked steaks release more moisture than fresh.

Hard Sear

  • Heat cast iron over high heat 4–5 min, until smoking.
  • Thin film of high–smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed).
  • Sear 45–60 sec per side. Interior is already at temp.
  • Optional: stand on fat cap edge ~30 sec with tongs.

Optional: Butter Baste

  • In the last 30 sec of searing, add a knob of butter, smashed garlic, sprig of thyme/rosemary.
  • Tilt pan, spoon foaming butter over steak.

Rest

  • Short rest — 2–3 min.
  • Pepper after resting.
Tip: this is the best no-gear, no-planning method. Start the oven ~2 hours before dinner and walk away. Surprisingly close to fresh in the final result.
