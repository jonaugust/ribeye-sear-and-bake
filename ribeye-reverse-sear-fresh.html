Oven Reverse Sear Ribeye (Fresh)

Vegetable Side

  • Skip the low oven phase — too cool to char. Toss bok choy (halved), broccolini, or asparagus into the cast iron during the steak's rest after the hard sear.
  • 2–3 min in the rendered fat = crispy, charred edges. Skip watery veg (zucchini, mushrooms).
Low oven first, hard sear at the end. Best method for thick steaks (1.5"+) — edge-to-edge pink, killer crust, no sous vide gear needed.

Prep (45 min – overnight before)

  • Pat steak very dry with paper towels.
  • Salt generously on both sides.
  • Place on a wire rack over a sheet pan. Leave uncovered.
  • Rest in the fridge 45 min minimum, ideally a few hours, up to overnight (dry-brine — even better crust).

Low and Slow in Oven

  • Preheat oven to 250°F.
  • Place steak on wire rack over sheet pan, insert thermometer probe.
  • Cook until internal temp is ~10–15°F below target (see below).

  • 1" steak: ~25–35 min.
  • 1.5" ribeye: ~40–50 min.
  • 2"+ steak: ~60–75 min.
  • Use the thermometer, not the clock.

Rest Briefly

  • Pull from oven. Let steak rest 5–10 min while you heat the skillet.
  • Pat the surface dry one more time before searing.

Hard Sear

  • Heat cast iron over high heat 4–5 min, until smoking.
  • Thin film of high–smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed).
  • Sear 45–60 sec per side. That's it — interior is already at temp.
  • Optional: stand on fat cap edge ~30 sec with tongs.

Optional: Butter Baste

  • In the last 30 sec of searing, add a knob of butter, smashed garlic, sprig of thyme/rosemary.
  • Tilt pan, spoon foaming butter over steak.

Rest

  • Short rest — 2–3 min (the long rest happened before the sear).
  • Pepper after resting.
Tip: target temp is lower than sear-and-bake because the hard sear at the end will raise the internal temp by ~10°F. The overnight dry-brine on a wire rack is the single biggest crust upgrade — worth planning ahead for.
